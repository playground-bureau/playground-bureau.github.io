2. Food and Beverages 

Food

The Bureau's Department of Delectable Meals, led by Chef Lotte and talented guest chefs, will be serving up delicious meals six times throughout the program, plus light breakfast every morning. This year the menu is fully vegan/vegetarian.

Dinner on Thursday, lunch and dinner on Friday/Saturday, and a big afternoon brunch on Sunday.

IMPORTANT: If you have any serious allergies, please inform us ASAP (if you haven't already done so when you purchased your ticket). You can reach us via email or instagram.

IMPORTANT 2: While lunch/dinner will be provided, we encourage you to bring your own snacks and sweets for those in-between cravings. The kitchen will not be available for public use.

Community Open Bar

The Bureau's Community Open Bar operates under a simple principle: you donate, we all drink. No need to pay 18 euros for a cocktail, or any euros for that matter.

In order to make this work, we kindly oblige you to bring beverages of your choice and gift them upon arrival to the bar. If you are a big drinker, bring big. If you are a light drinker, bring light. Everything will even out. Everyone will get to enjoy your contribution, and you'll enjoy theirs. The Bureau will also be buying essentials like water and soft drinks as well as beers, garnish, and some liquor to make standard cocktails. The bartenders will then combine the donated drinks and serve you something special.

It can be anything: beer, a bottle of wine, three bottles of wine, gin, whisky, whisky + your favorite mixer, some bizarre niche liqueur, kombucha, you name it. 

3. Getting There

Shuttle (Bureau Transit Division)

We plan to make a shuttle bus from Barcelona available to you, but this will be announced a couple months before the event. Keep your eyes on the right channels. 

Driving

If you are driving over, no problem at all, you will be able to park your car. But we don't want every participant driving up solo when you can carpool! We will be coordinating carpooling in the week leading up to the program, so stay tuned if you have free spots in your vehicle or if you wish to hitch a ride.

The drive from Barcelona city center is about 2 hours.

Train

Alternatively, you can board the train from Plaça Espanya and ride it to Girona or Figueres station (1h30–2h00). From there, you can catch a taxi to the venue. The taxi fare should be approximately 50–60 euros.

Gates & Arrival Times

We highly recommend grabbing Thursday afternoon off. Gates open 14:00 Thursday. Arrive before sunset for easier tent-pitching and an early start. Thursday will begin with our usual Opening Act—you don't want to miss it! 

We really want to avoid congestion near the entrance portal during peak arrival times on Thursday. 19:00–20:00 will likely be rush hour. It's not fun for us, and it won't be fun for you either if you have to wait. Leave work early if you can, or have your parents call in sick to your office.

IMPORTANT: The gates will be closed Saturday for arrivals.

4. Accommodation

Camping

Designated camping areas are available onsite with plenty of space for your tent. Camping areas are situated at a sufficient distance from music stages to permit rest. If you don't have a tent, feel free to ask in the WhatsApp group—someone might have a spare.

IMPORTANT: RVs/vans are also welcome, but please let us know via email beforehand since capacity is limited.

Lodgings

The site offers a selection of lodgings, ranging from charming cabin to extremely charming mountain-view lodges. We will release tickets for these at a later date. Keep an eye on the WhatsApp Community Group if you want a warm bed and roof to rest your weary bones after long nights of dancing.

5. On Site

Showers & Bathrooms

The site is equipped with showers and bathrooms, complete with mirrors to help you get festival-ready. Don't forget to bring your own towel and toiletries!

Money

The whole idea is that you won't have to spend money once you're at the program—meals and open bar are included. However, there may be a few optional areas where cash or transfer transactions could take place. In past years, we've had massage, tattooing, and a special ethnobotanical mixology bar. We'll let you know what's on the menu this year :)

Phone Guidelines

Disconnect from your phone, reconnect with actual humans. The scenery is photo-worthy, but no unsolicited pics of people mid-boogie. Think Berlin nightclub rules: no photos on the dancefloor.

A photographer will be on-site to capture moments worth framing.

Most carriers have no reception here, by the way. Let your family and friends know you'll be off the grid so they don't worry.

Substance Use

We hope this goes without saying, but we prioritize safety and respect for all participants. While we acknowledge some may choose to partake in substances, we urge you to do so responsibly. Use common sense and help keep the vibes positive and safe.

6. Bureau Policy

We state it clearly on our website, but this is a friendly reminder: we do not tolerate any discrimination based on gender, sexuality, or identity, nor any form of harassment—sexual or otherwise. We reserve the right to remove anyone from our premises.

Dedicated staff will be available to address any concerns that may arise, alongside security to handle inappropriate or harmful behaviour. If you witness or experience something that doesn't feel right, please let us know.

We will always respect your privacy and your choices.

Schedule & Teardown

The program kicks off on Thursday, September 24th at 14:00, and wraps up on Sunday, September 27th at 18:00.

From Sunday 18:00 onwards, we will start the teardown—if you stick around, you will be legally obligated to lend a hand. (This is not a joke. Bureau Directive 4.1(a).)

7. Packing Guide

Cups, Plates & Utensils

We kindly ask you to BYOPCU (Bring Your Own Plate, Cup, and Utensils). If you are flying in from abroad or have other reasons why this is difficult for you, please let us know so we can plan accordingly.

WHAT TO PACK:

  • Your ID
  • A cup
  • Plate + cutlery / something to eat out of/with
  • BEVERAGES TO DONATE to the community bar (more info above)
  • Warm clothes! It's 12–14 degrees at night.
  • Toiletries—there are showers on site!
  • Caring, open, and silly mindset.
  • Swimwear

CAMPING GEAR:

  • Tent, sleeping bag / cosy bedding
  • Torch
  • Rain protection, rain coat (just in case!)
  • Your favourite water bottle
  • Mosquito repellent
  • Mosquito repellent (saying it twice because it's important)
  • TOWEL / TOOTHBRUSH

NICE-TO-HAVES:

  • Floaties 
  • Vitamins (C, B-12, Magnesium)—it will help with your hangovers!
  • Festival outfits

THINGS NOT TO PACK:

  • Fireworks
  • Surströmming
