What’s Included in your ticket?

  • Food

    The Bureau's Department of Feeding Hungry Souls, led by Chef Lotte and talented guest chefs, will be serving up delicious meals six times throughout the festival. This year we have decided to go full vegan/vegetarian, we will not serve meat based options. Dinner on Thursday, lunch and dinner on Friday/Saturday, as well as a big ole' afternoon brunch on Sunday.

    IMPORTANT: If you have any serious allergies, please inform us ASAP (if you haven’t already done so when you purchased your ticket). You can reach out to us via email or instagram.

    IMPORTANT 2: While lunch/dinner will be provided, we encourage you to bring your own snacks and sweets for those in-between cravings as well as light breakfasts. The kitchen will not be available for public use.

  • Community Open Bar

    The Playground Bureau thrives on community spirit. We’ve set up a Community Open Bar. Yup, that's right, no need to pay 18 euros for a cocktail, or any euros for that matter. All drinks are on the house, or rather, on the community!

    In order to make this work, we kindly ask you to bring a couple bottles of your choice and gift it upon arrival to the bar. If you are a big drinker, bring big. If you are a light drinker, bring light. Everything will even out. 
    Everyone will get to enjoy your contribution, and you’ll enjoy theirs. The Bureau will also be buying essentials like water and soft drinks as well as beers, garnish, and some liquor to make standard cocktails. The bartenders will then combine the donated drinks and serve you something special.

    There's a niche liquer you love that you want to share? Bring a bottle! Fan of good-old Estrella? That's cool too, bring a 6 pack :). You are a non-alcoholic drinker? Soft-drinks like kombucha are also very welcome! Let's create an eclectic bar together.

    Reminder: An open bar can be a slippery slope, so please drink responsibly. We want you to have fun, but not get shibuya-meltdown-drunk.


  • Music & Workshops

    There will be dance music provided for you all night long by our talented pool of DJs and Artists. The camping area is pretty quiet at night, allowing you to either go bananas till sunrise or get a decent night's sleep—the choice is yours! We will have an eclectic mix of activities both during the day and night. Day time will generally be more relaxed, allowing you to learn, hang out, make new friends, and enjoy the beautiful Catalan nature.

  • Cups, plates, and utensils

    Unlike last year, where the venue had enough plates and utensils to serve an entire festival, we are a bit limited this year. Therefore, we kindly ask you to BYOPCU (bring your own plate, cup, and utensils). If you are flying in from abroad or have other reasons why this is difficult for you, please let us know so we have a good idea of how much crockery we have to provide. 

  • Money

    The idea is that you won't have to spend money once you are at the festival with meals and open bar included. However, there are a few areas where cash and transfer transactions could take place. Last year, we had massage, tattooing, and a special ethnobotanical mixology bar. We'll soon know what's on the menu for this year :)

  • WhatsApp Group

    Join the Community Whatsapp Group.  Whether you need a ride, a tent, outfit recommendations, or a hot take on Félix Guattari's seminal work Anti-Oedipus: Capitalism and Schizophrenia (1972), the group is at your disposal! Just don't spam it too much, okay?

  • Camping

    Our beautiful campsite offers plenty of space for your tent. If you don’t have one, feel free to ask in the WhatsApp group—someone might have a spare.

    IMPORTANT: RVs are also welcome, but please let us know beforehand since capacity is limited.

  • Lodgings

    Our beautiful site offers a selection of lodgings, ranging from charming cabin to extremely charming mountain view lodges. We will release the tickets sales for these at a later date. So keep an eye on the Whatsapp Community Group if you want a warm bed and roof to rest your weary bones after long nights of dancing. 

    IMPORTANT: RVs are also welcome, but please let us know beforehand since capacity is limited.

  • Access & Arrivals

    If you are driving over, no problem at all, you will be able to park your car. But, of course, we don't want every participant driving up solo when you can carpool! We will be coordinating carpooling in the week leading up to the festival, so stay tuned if you have free spots in your vehicle or if you wish to hitch a ride.

    The drive from Barcelona city center is about 2 hour.

    Alternatively, you can board the train from Plaça Espanya and ride it to Girona or Figueres station (1h30~2h00 hours). From there, you can catch a taxi to the venue. The taxi fare should be approximately 50-60 euros.

  • Gates opening hours :

    We highly recommend grabbing Thursday afternoon off. Gates open 14:00 Thursday. Arrive before sunset for easier tent-pitching and an early start on the goodtimes. Friday arrivals are fine… but why miss an extra night of lakeside monkeying?

    IMPORTANT :     The gates will be closed Saturday for arrivals.

  • Showers and Bathrooms

    The site is equipped with showers and bathrooms, complete with mirrors to help you get festival-ready. REMINDER: Don’t forget to bring your own towel and toiletries!

  • Outfits : Saturday Green Day 

    We’re committed to making our festival as green as possible — and on Saturday, we’ll be taking this literally.
    All clothes and costumes are welcome. The only rule: wear something (or everything) GREEN. Shirts, hats, socks, sunglasses. Emerald, lime, olive, mint, forest, sage — all welcome. Dress as a frog, a cucumber, or yes… even in a Green Day T-shirt. Just keep it green!

  • Substance Use

    We hope this goes without saying, but we prioritize safety and respect for all attendees. While we acknowledge some may choose to partake in substances, we urge you to do so responsibly. Use common sense and help keep the vibes positive and safe.

    We especially don't encourage the excessive use of Charlie, as it's just... not quite our vibe. We kindly ask that, if you do choose to partake, you do so discreetly and respectfully for yourself and others.

    The Tea House will be a dedicated area where you will be able to rest, relax, regroup or just hang out in case you need a small break.

  • Our Policy

    We state it clearly on our website, but this is a friendly reminder: we do not tolerate any discrimination based on gender, sexuality, or identity, nor any form of harassment—sexual or otherwise. We reserve the right to remove anyone from our premises.

    Dedicated staff will be available to address any concerns that may arise, alongside security to handle inappropriate or harmful behaviour. If you witness or experience something that doesn’t feel right, please let us know.

    We will always respect your privacy and your choices.

  • Schedule

    The festival kicks off on Thursday, September 24th at 2 PM, and wraps up on Sunday, September 27th at 6PM.

    We really want to avoid congestion near the entrance portal during peak arrival times on Thursday. 7-8PM will likely be rush hour. It's not fun for us, and it won't be fun for you either if you have to wait to get let in. Leave work early if you can, or have your parents call in sick to your office and come join us in the afternoon.

    From Sunday 6PM onwards, we will start the teardown—if you stick around, you will be legally obligated to lend a hand.

  • Phone Guidelines & Recommendations

    Disconnect from your phone, reconnect with actual humans. The scenery is photo-worthy, but no unsolicited pics of people mid-boogie. Think Berlin nightclub rules: no photos on the dancefloor.

    A photographer will be on-site to capture moments worth framing.

    Most carriers have no reception here — blissfully remote. If you must, offline messaging apps like Bitchat can help ping friends… but we recommend going full 70s style and just wandering until you find them.

    Let your family and friends know you’ll be off the grid so they don’t worry.
