History of the Playground Bureau

1. Origins
In 1980, Catherine “Cat” Sandwich — a previously unknown perennial candidate with no political experience, no major endorsements, and a campaign budget composed largely of IOUs — defeated Ronald Reagan in a landslide presidential election. Her platform was simple: the government should stop measuring how much money everyone is making and start measuring how good of a time everyone is having.
Within her first hundred days, President Sandwich signed the Goodtimes Governance Act, replacing Gross Domestic Product with a new national metric: Goodtimesium — a quantum-chemical-neurological phenomenon emitted by human beings in states of genuine connection, joy, and collective silliness. Goodtimesium is not manufactured, purchased, or consumed. It is radiated. The more of it you emit, the more the people around you emit. It spreads like radioactive glitter.
To oversee this new national priority, the Sandwich administration elevated a humble municipal parks office into a full government agency: the Playground Bureau, operating under the newly established Ministry of Happiness. Our mandate was, and remains, the research, measurement, and elevation of Goodtimesium levels across the general population.
2. The Separatoxin Problem
It wasn’t long before Bureau scientists identified a counterforce. Where Goodtimesium draws people together, Separatoxin drives them apart — a mysterious phenomenon spreading through the population, making people hang out less, stay home more, and spend increasing amounts of time alone. The two forces repel each other. Where Separatoxin concentrations rise, Goodtimesium levels collapse, and vice versa.
In recent decades, Separatoxin levels have reached epidemic proportions. Citizens are advised to keep social distancing to a minimum, contact loved ones regularly, and organize picnics where possible. The Bureau’s core mission — elevating Goodtimesium — has never been more urgent.
3. The Syndicate
In 2005, a group of Bureau agents broke away to form a renegade splinter cell known as the Syndicate. The circumstances of the split remain classified: [REDACTED BY BUREAU DEPARTMENT OF INTERNAL AFFAIRS — FILE UNDER REVIEW — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE]. What is known is that the Syndicate considers the Bureau’s methods “too slow” and “too soft on corporations Goodtimes-washing their shady operations.” They wear tinfoil hats to insulate from what they call “7G radiation.” They operate unlicensed Separatoxin detectors. They get around on modded tricycles.
The Bureau’s official position on the Syndicate is that they are a nuisance and a regulatory headache. Unofficially, some of them are our friends and we miss them. But you didn’t hear that from us.
4. Corporate Interests
Carrefoogle Corporation is a multinational conglomerate spanning consumer electronics, energy, defense, oil tanker insurance, yogurt, blockchain, food, water, air, and companion AI. They have expressed ongoing interest in the Bureau’s work and have served as private sector partner for several Bureau programs, including MONKEY BUSINESS.
The Syndicate alleges that Carrefoogle is either contributing to Separatoxin levels or profiting from them. Carrefoogle categorically denies this. The Bureau takes no official position on the matter, as per Sponsorship Agreement Section 11.4(a).
5. Bureau Operations Today
We get it — try elevating Goodtimesium levels while following all 2,179 regulatory protocols. Far cry from our days as a humble parks office. We’ve got quarterly glitter quotas to meet, surprise inspections from the Department of Just Making Sure You’re Staying Hydrated Pal, and twelve signatures required just to make the disco balls spin the other way. But we wouldn’t trade it for anything.
Our flagship annual program, MONKEY BUSINESS, gathers willing participants in conditions designed to elevate Goodtimesium emissions through communal meals, music, workshops, performances, dancing, laughing, and the kind of conversations you only have at 3 AM with someone you met six hours ago. The goal is for participants to carry elevated levels home and spread them outward — like radioactive glitter.
The Playground Bureau remains committed to its founding mandate. Separatoxin levels are rising. The work is urgent. But the methods are joyful, and the results — when they hit — are unmistakable. You know it when you feel it.
