Pre-Registration Briefing (continued)

1. Respect & Consent
This should go without saying, but at the Bureau, respect and consent is absolutely essential. We welcome guests of any gender, race, ethnicity, sexuality, age (above 18), class identity, political beliefs, hair style, dance move preferences, you name it. Everyone should be able to express themselves fully, feeling free to engage in creative, collaborative participation. We have a ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY for any kind of harassment, and when you commit to buying a ticket, we trust that you understand and support this outlook. Similarly, we do encourage you all to go bonkers, but know your limits. Make sure your fun doesn't come at someone else's expense. Basically, don't be a fu          [REDACTED BY BUREAU DEPARTMENT OF APPROPRIATE LANGUAGE STANDARDS].
2. Site Preservation
The site is gorgeous, and we aim to keep it that way—both for ourselves and for the ones who follow (and also so we don't lose our deposit).
  • Bins will be everywhere, so use them. Everything that is waste must end up in them.
  • Cigarette butts are a serious fire hazard in the region on top of being harmful for the environment. Bring a tin or an ashtray. If you need one, we will provide one.
  • No open flames. This includes candles, BBQs, fireworks. Leave them at home. Small camping stoves are allowed.
3. Legal Entity & Membership
The Playground Bureau is a non-profit cultural association (under the name Associació per a la Promoció dels Enllaços Socials — A.P.E.S.), and therefore all proceeds are reinvested into the community to foster creativity and build new events and ideas. Since this gathering is formally a private members-only event, your ticket fee is actually a membership contribution to our association — by securing your spot, you will automatically be registered as a member of the association until the end of the festival. (Hence why we will ask you for your ID number during registration.)
