Hi, hello, we are once again beyond THRILLED to announce the third iteration of the Bureau's annual four-day program dedicated to the research, testing, and refinement of methods for increasing Goodtimesium levels in the general population.
The program gathers participants at a rural site for a kontrolled environment in which various approaches to Goodtimesium elevation are trialed, including communal meals, deejay and live music, workshops, performances, comedy, theatrics, laughing, dancing, making old friends out of new ones, having an ice-cold drink by the lake under big blue skies, waking up to chirping birds, and much more. We can't wait to welcome you there!
As per Sponsorship Agreement Section 9.2b, we are obliged to state here that we are "super excited!" to have Carrefoogle Corporation as our official event sponsor. Previous participants may remember them for their sponsoring of the Scrolling Area (designated scrolling booth designed to prevent second-hand scrolling) during the last two Monkey Businesses.
All provisions, including food, beverages*, and programming, are included in the registration contribution. No monetary transactions take place onsite during the event.
* The bar is free, but participants are required to donate drinks to keep it stocked. See Beverage Provisions below.
** Carrefoogle Corporation wishes us to communicate that they are deeply committed to Goodtimesium elevation, and categorically deny all allegations of ‘goodtimes-washing’ and links to rising Separatoxin levels.

Registration for MB-3 has not yet opened. When it does, it will open in tiers, released on dates that will be published on this page and through official Bureau communication channels. No additional expenditure is required onsite. Please leave your wallet in your tent, your car, or ideally at home. Its presence has been shown to reduce Goodtimesium levels by up to 14%.

5. Food Provisions
Meals are prepared onsite by the Bureau's Department of Feeding Hungry Souls, a volunteer unit composed of professional and hobbyist chefs. Multiple meal services are provided over the duration of the program. Participants are advised to supplement with personal supplies such as light snacks and breakfast items for periods when the kitchen is not in service.
6. Beverage Provisions
A communal bar stocked with water, soft drinks, spirits, and beer is maintained throughout the program at no additional cost. Participants are encouraged to contribute one bottle of their preferred beverage to the communal supply. Responsible consumption is expected of all participants.
7. Accommodation
Designated camping areas are available onsite. Participants should bring their own tent. Camping areas are situated at a sufficient distance from music stages to permit rest. Vehicle camping is also permitted. Additional lodging options, if available, will be announced closer to the program date.
8. Site Access & Transportation
The program site is located in rural Catalunya. Detailed access instructions, including exact coordinates and transportation options, will be communicated to registered participants. Information regarding any Bureau-organized shuttle services will be published on this page when confirmed.
